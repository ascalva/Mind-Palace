# Distillation — superconnections and their curvature

**Load-bearing result (what our corpus relies on).** Quillen (1985) introduces the notion of
a **superconnection**: on a `Z/2`-graded (super) vector bundle, a superconnection generalizes
an ordinary connection by allowing odd-degree components that mix the graded pieces, not
just the usual covariant-derivative piece. Its **curvature** — the superconnection squared —
is likewise `Z/2`-graded and, when the underlying complex is not exact, has a nonzero
component measuring the failure of the complex to be a genuine chain complex with vanishing
differential-squared; Quillen uses this curvature to build explicit Chern-character
representatives. Non-vanishing curvature is thus a **well-defined, computable obstruction**
to flatness, with its own geometric meaning (a characteristic class), not merely "the
construction failed."
